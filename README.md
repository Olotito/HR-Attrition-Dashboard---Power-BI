# HR-Attrition-Dashboard-Power-BI

## 📊 Why are Employees Leaving?

I analyzed HR data from 1,470 employees and built an interactive HR Attrition Dashboard in Power BI to identify key drivers of employee turnover.

<img width="870" height="483" alt="Performance 004" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/93e5950b-a6f2-4359-842c-dbda4179aebc" />


## Key Findings
- **Attrition Rate: 16.12%**
- **Highest turnover:** Employees aged 26-35 
- **Department risk:** Sales recorded the highest attrition rate
- **Travel impact:** Frequent business travel was associated with higher employee turnover
- **Satisfaction link:** Job satisfaction appears to influence employee retention

## Skills & Tools Used
- **Power Query:** Data extraction & transformation
- **Data Cleaning:** Handled missing values & inconsistencies 
- **Data Modeling:** Built relationships between HR tables
- **DAX:** Created measures for Attrition Rate, Avg Income, KPIs
- **KPI Design:** Total Employees, Attrition %, Avg Age, Avg Monthly Income
- **Data Visualization:** Bar charts, line graphs, donut charts, slicers

## Dashboard Features
1. **KPI Cards:** Total Employees 1470, Attrition 16.12%, Avg Age 36.92, Avg Monthly Income $6.5K
2. **Slicers:** Department, Gender, Education, Job Role for dynamic filtering
3. **Attrition Analysis:** By Department, Age Group, Job Satisfaction, Business Travel
4. **Compensation View:** Monthly Income by Job Role

## Business Impact
This dashboard enables HR teams to:
1. Identify high-risk departments and age groups
2. Quantify impact of business travel on retention 
3. Correlate job satisfaction with attrition rates
4. Make data-driven retention strategies
